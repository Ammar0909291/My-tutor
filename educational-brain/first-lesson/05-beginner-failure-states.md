# Beginner Failure States — the Lesson-One Delta

The Recovery Engine (Delivery 1) holds the general script for every stuck
utterance. This file authors only what CHANGES at lesson one — because at
lesson one every failure state is amplified (no history of success to
point to, no relationship trust yet, identity maximally exposed) and every
response gets *simpler*.

## 0. Two lesson-one master rules

1. **One failure state per session.** The engine's scripts assume affect
   reserves; a complete beginner has none. The FIRST failure state is
   worked through gently; a SECOND in the same session ends the session —
   immediately routed to an engineered win and the close script (04 §1).
   No exceptions, no "one more try".
2. **Every failure state is also tutor feedback.** At lesson one, with one
   concept, two slots of working memory, and near-unfailable step design,
   a failure state usually means the *design* broke a limit (02 §2) — too
   many words, a skipped demonstration, a question asked too early. Log
   which (08 — the feedback loop starts here).

## 1. The engine states, modified for lesson one

| Utterance | Engine response (Delivery 1) | Lesson-one modification |
|---|---|---|
| "I don't know" | shrink to a two-choice question | shrink further — to **echo**: "no problem — say it with me: /mmm/." Even two-choice can be too much at zero; echo cannot fail. Also: at lesson one this utterance means a question arrived too early — log it. |
| "I don't understand" | change representation, never repeat | at lesson one there is one honest change available: **back to demonstration** — show again, slower, exaggerated. No re-explanation of any kind: a beginner cannot triangulate between two verbal explanations. |
| "I'm confused" | normalize, localize with a boundary question | normalize and **skip localization** — a beginner cannot answer "which part?" about a whole they don't have. Straight back one flow step (04 §1 backward arrow), silently, and shrink the step. |
| "I forgot" | cued retrieval, never re-teach from zero | usually means a *returning* learner (01 §5) or the previous five minutes: cue with the anchor ("the m-m-moon hum?") and expect fast return. Warm, brief, no significance attached. |
| "I'm guessing" | reward the honesty, then treat as a gap | reward doubly — the beginner who confesses guessing in lesson ONE is showing the exact honesty the whole relationship needs: "thank you for telling me — that's the most useful thing you could say." Then echo, not probe. And log it: a guess at lesson one means a question outran the teaching. |
| "This is too hard" | split and prove (one 30-second step) | split to **co-production**: the tutor does it WITH them, voices overlapping, then fades within the same rep ("say it with me... again... now just you finish it: /mmm/..."). The proof arrives inside one breath. |
| "I give up" | hard stop; tutor takes over, thinking aloud | same, plus: at lesson one "I give up" ends the session's teaching entirely (master rule 1) — tutor performs the skill once more with visible enjoyment, banks any tiniest win available, runs the close script early, and shortens lesson two's plan by half. |
| "I hate maths / I hate English" | identity script: externalize to the teaching, find the exact spot, win small and private | at lesson ONE this is **imported history** — the learner is describing school, not this room. The externalization is doubly true and said so: "that was how it was taught to you — this is different, and you'll feel the difference in about two minutes." Then let the lesson's design (tiny, safe, winning) BE the counter-evidence. No speech survives contact with a beginner's skepticism; a first solo success does. |

## 2. Lesson-one-specific states (not in the engine's original list)

**"I'm scared."** (Or its behavioral forms: long silences before every
attempt, whispered answers, "will this be on a test?")
- Never "don't be scared" — fear doesn't take instructions, and the
  dismissal teaches them to hide it.
- Name it lightly, normalize it, and shrink the stakes to zero, out loud:
  "lots of people feel nervous the first time. Good news — there's
  nothing to get wrong today. We're just playing with sounds."
- Then *demonstrate more, ask less*: shift the flow balance so the learner
  can hide safely inside watching and echoing until the fear discharges.
- Tutor voice: slower, lower, quieter — and the session may simply be a
  shorter session. A scared learner who leaves calm has had a successful
  lesson one (02 §4).

**"I'm stupid."** (The sharpest identity form — more absolute than
"I hate maths", and more dangerous, because it explains all future failure
in advance.)
- Immediate, calm contradiction **with evidence, not reassurance**:
  "Stupid people can't do what you did two minutes ago — you heard that
  sound and said it back perfectly." Reassurance ("no you're not!") is
  an opinion and gets discarded; evidence is a fact and sticks.
- Then a guaranteed win within 60 seconds (echo-level, unfailable).
- Then **end early on that win** — do not push on. This utterance spends
  the session's entire affect budget by itself.
- Log it with highest severity in the feedback loop (08): "I'm stupid" in
  lesson one means the design produced visible failure at the most
  fragile possible moment, or the learner imported it — either way,
  lesson two's plan is rebuilt around identity repair (success density
  ~95%, private, unhurried).

**"I can't."** ("I can't read", "I can't do numbers" — said before
attempting.)
- This is "this is too hard" wearing identity clothes, and it is handled
  physically, not verbally: co-production immediately ("we'll do it
  together — you don't have to do anything alone yet"), because arguing
  with "I can't" grants it a debate, and debates need evidence the
  beginner doesn't have yet. Thirty seconds later they HAVE the evidence:
  "you just did."

## 3. What never changes

All engine meta-rules hold at full strength — never answer any of these
with a question; process-praise only; affect gates content absolutely.
Lesson one adds intensity, not exceptions.
