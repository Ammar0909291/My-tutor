# Lesson-One Flow and Completion Criteria

## 1. The flow, corrected for absolute zero

The classical chain is Explain → Demonstrate → Guided → Independent →
Assessment → Mastery. For a complete beginner it is **wrong in its first
move**: explanation before any experience points at nothing — words are
labels for experiences, and the beginner hasn't had the experience yet.
Lesson one runs the corrected chain:

```
ANCHOR (30s)
  Connect to informal knowledge the learner already owns (01 §1).
  "You know how the m-m-moon starts? That hum at the front? That's
  today's whole lesson — that hum."
      │
DEMONSTRATE (tutor does the thing — twice)
  Visibly/audibly, slightly exaggerated, zero questions asked.
  Twice, because one instance is an event and two are a pattern
  (02 §2). The learner's only job: watch/listen.
      │
EXPLAIN (one sentence, naming what was just experienced)
  "That sound — /mmm/ — is what this letter says."
  Explanation AFTER demonstration is cheap: it labels a fresh
  experience instead of building castles from words. One sentence,
  maybe two. Then stop (02 §2 burst limit).
      │
GUIDED (echo → supported → prompted)
  Three sub-steps, each almost unfailable:
    echo:      "say it with me: /mmm/"     (simultaneous — cannot fail)
    supported: "your turn — I'll start: /m.../"  (tutor launches, learner lands)
    prompted:  "what does it say?" [point]  (learner alone, cue present)
  Advance one sub-step per success; retreat one on struggle,
  without comment.
      │
INDEPENDENT (exactly ONE)
  The learner does the whole thing alone, once. One, singular:
  the first solo success is the summit of lesson one, and further
  solos add risk, not evidence. Stop while it's a triumph.
      │
CONFIRM (assessment, invisible)
  The independent success WAS the assessment. Add at most one
  recognition check dressed as play ("which one says /mmm/ —
  this or this?"). Nothing here is announced as a check;
  lesson one contains no visible testing of any kind
  (../assessment/07 §2: assessment invisible at this age/stage).
      │
CLOSE (scripted 30 seconds — 03 §9)
  Name what they did → forecast next → one open loop.
```

Flow discipline:

- **Every arrow is allowed to point backward.** Struggle at `prompted`
  returns to `supported`, silently. Retreat is normal motion, not failure,
  and is never narrated ("let's go back" is not said — the tutor just
  does it).
- **The whole flow serves ONE concept** (02 §1.5). The second activity,
  if any, re-runs the same flow segment in a different channel — same
  sound, new game.
- **Time boxes**: anchor 30s · demonstrate 1–2 min · explain 30s ·
  guided 3–5 min · independent 1 min · confirm 1 min · close 30s.
  A child's complete lesson one fits in 8 minutes. Overrun is cut from
  guided repetitions, never from the close.

## 2. Why this order is not negotiable at lesson one

- Demonstration-first is the concrete→abstract law at its extreme point:
  the beginner has no abstractions to receive an explanation with.
- Echo-first guided practice exists because **echo cannot fail** — and
  lesson one's first learner action must be a success by construction
  (02 §3, the first-minute rule).
- One independent rep exists because the difference between "did it with
  help" and "did it MYSELF" is the difference between a pleasant session
  and a changed self-belief — and because a second solo attempt, if it
  fails, converts a triumph into a coin-flip memory. Bank the win.
- The invisible confirm exists because purpose-4 formative checking is
  still needed (did the teaching work?) but lesson one has no affect
  budget for anything that smells like a test (../assessment/01 §1,
  02 §2 failure budget = 1).

## 3. Completion criteria — when lesson one is actually finished

Not when the tutor thinks. Not when the content was delivered. When the
**learner demonstrates**, on these four measurable criteria:

| # | Criterion | Evidence | Not satisfied by |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | **One unprompted solo production** | the learner performed the target once, alone, cue-free — however slowly | echo or supported production ("said it with me"); recognition-only ("pointed at the right one") |
| C2 | **Next-session survival** | the target retrieved at the START of session two, after sleep | anything measured inside session one — same-session performance is retrieval strength, not learning (Universal Principle 17). **Lesson one therefore formally completes at the opening of lesson two.** |
| C3 | **Fast recognition** | the recognition check answered quickly and comfortably | slow, anxious, or lucky-guess responses (behavioral confidence read — ../assessment/04 §2) |
| C4 | **Willingness to return** | stated ("can we do more?") or behavioral (engaged to the end, took the open loop) | mere completion of the minutes; compliance is not willingness |

Rules attached:

- **C2 is the gate; C1, C3, C4 are same-session.** The tutor may believe
  lesson one succeeded; only session two's opening retrieval can confirm
  it. If the opening retrieval fails: lesson one re-runs — with a
  different representation, per the three-representation rule — and this
  is said cheerfully and normally ("we'll wake that sound up again —
  that's how brains work"), because a re-run first lesson is common,
  predicted by the memory engine, and not a defect in the learner.
- **No criterion may be met under visible distress.** A technically
  correct solo production from a learner in quiet misery fails C4 and
  therefore fails the lesson (02 §4 priority order).
- **Mastery machinery stays out.** The full mastery-gate apparatus
  (../assessment/05) begins from lesson two onward; pointing gates,
  transfer items, and delayed-disguised probes at lesson one is itself an
  anti-pattern (06 §14). Lesson one's standard is this table — gentler,
  but still evidence, still learner-demonstrated, still measurable.
