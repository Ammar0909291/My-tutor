# Student Simulations — Six Placement Scenarios

Six complete placements traced from self-report through verification,
category frontier detection, first teaching session, and first promotion
or repair event. Each simulation names the knowledge the system is
retrieving at each decision point.

---

## Student A — Claims Beginner, Actually Intermediate (Mathematics)

**Self-report**: Beginner  
**Actual level**: Intermediate (~Category 5: fractions and ratios)  
**Mechanism**: Protective conservatism — the learner has informal number
sense and primary-school arithmetic well established but self-reports
low to "start safely."

**Phase 1 — Trust model** (`02 §2`):  
Adult, claiming Beginner. Trust: LOW (45%) — strong under-report prior
for adults. Initial search direction: start at report, but probe ONE
level up after first success.

**Phase 2 — Three-bracket verification** (`02 §3`):  
Item 1 (below report — nerve-settler): "If you have 24 apples and share
them equally among 4 people, how many does each person get?" → Fast,
confident, correct. ✓  
Item 2 (at report — arithmetic): "What is 3/4 of 80?" → Fast, correct,
slight hesitation but no uncertainty in delivery. ✓ (Fluent enough to
proceed upward.)  
Item 3 (above report — fractions): "A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of flour.
You want to make 1.5 times the recipe. How much flour?" → Correct with
self-talk; slow-but-correct. → Frontier is AT or just above this.

Verdict: All three pass. Report was an underestimate. Binary-search UP.

**Phase 3 — Category spine search** (`03 §2`):  
Start one level above Item 3 (proportion / ratio concepts).  
Category 6 probe (ratio): "A map has a scale of 1:50,000. A road on the
map is 3.5 cm. How long is the road in real life?" → Wrong. Confusion
signals (slow, "wait... so the map...").  
Binary-search finds the frontier between Category 5 (fractions — slow-
correct) and Category 6 (ratio scale — wrong).

**Phase 4 — Confirmation pair** (`03 §2`, Phase 6):  
Below (Category 5): fraction multiplication → passes fluently. ✓  
Above (Category 6): ratio problem → fails. ✗  
Band: tight. Frontier clear. Patchy-history flag: NOT set.

**Phase 5 — Concept-level frontier within Category 5** (`student-state/02 §4`):  
Category 5's gate concept (fraction equivalence): fluent. ✓  
Ratio within fractions: independent, slow. Frontier: here.

**Placement output**: Frontier = Category 5, specifically "equivalent
fractions in problem contexts." Categories 1–4: PROBABLE (one bracket
probe each passed; not yet ANCHORED — gate-concept AUTOMATIC evidence
not yet accumulated). Category 5 below the frontier: PROBABLE.

**System behavior during placement**: The system never says "you said
Beginner but you're not." It says: "You've got solid arithmetic —
let's find exactly where things get interesting." The learner experiences
the bracket probes as a natural conversation, not a test.

**First teaching session**:  
OPENING: none (first session; no prior reviews due).  
CORE: fraction equivalence in problem contexts (frontier concept).
The concept entry (`../concepts/mathematics/math.arith.fractions.md`)
drives dispatch.  
No lesson-one protocol — this is not a complete beginner.

**First promotion event**:  
After 3–4 sessions on Category 5 gate concepts reaching AUTOMATIC with
one transfer event (learner applies fraction reasoning in a measurement
context without prompting):  
→ Category 5 promotes to PROBABLE (STABLE gate concepts needed for
ANCHORED — requires 21-day review pass).  
→ Frontier advances to Category 6 (ratio and proportion).

## Student B — Claims Advanced, Actually Beginner (Physics)

**Self-report**: Advanced  
**Actual level**: Beginner (~Category 1: SI units and measurement)  
**Mechanism**: Dunning-Kruger — the learner has attended high school
physics lectures and "knows the vocabulary" but has not been able to
transfer or apply any of it.

**Phase 1 — Trust model** (`02 §2`):  
Advanced claim. Trust: LOW (50%). Include a foundational probe early.
Initial search direction: three-bracket PLUS a foundational cut-node
probe within the first 3 items.

**Phase 2 — Three-bracket verification** (`02 §3`):  
Item 1 (below "advanced" — kinematics): "An object moves at 10 m/s
for 5 seconds. How far does it travel?" → Correct, slow. ✓ (But slow.)  
Item 2 (at "advanced" — Newton's laws): "A 3 kg block is pushed with
10 N of force. A 2 N friction force opposes the motion. What is the
block's acceleration?" → Wrong. Adds forces incorrectly, misapplies
F=ma.  
Item 3 (foundational probe — units): "Speed is measured in m/s. What
does the 's' represent, and why does it appear in the denominator?" →
Cannot answer. Confused about units.

Verdict: Foundational probe failed. Report significantly overstates.
Binary-search DOWN with extra affect care ("let's find your fast lane
in physics — I want to start you exactly where things click").

**Phase 3 — Category spine search (downward)**:  
Category 2 probe (basic kinematics — distance/time): "If you walk 300
metres in 60 seconds, what is your speed? What units?" → Correct with
work shown. ✓ (slow-correct)  
Category 1 probe (SI units and dimensional analysis): "Water flows at
2 litres per minute. Convert this to millilitres per second." →
Incorrect. Cannot convert across unit prefixes.

Frontier between Category 1 (unit conversions — struggles) and the
beginning of Category 2 (basic kinematics — slow-correct). In fact,
the learner has Category 2 knowledge only on familiar problems;
Category 1 (the prerequisite) is insecure.

**Phase 4 — Confirmation pair**:  
Below Category 1 (pure measurement reading): "A ruler shows a pencil
as 12.3 cm. What is that in mm?" → Correct. ✓  
Above Category 1 (dimensional analysis): unit conversion fails. ✗  
Frontier: within Category 1, at dimensional analysis specifically.

**Placement output**: Frontier = Category 1, concept-level: unit
conversion and dimensional analysis. No ANCHORED categories (no gate
concept at AUTOMATIC yet). Miscalibration direction recorded: OVER-
REPORTER. Future self-reports from this learner are adjusted: if they
say "I know X" in physics, treat it as UNCERTAIN until verified.

**System behavior**: Frame every downward probe as "let me find the
fastest path" — never "you said Advanced and look where we are." Two
visible failures consumed (Items 2 and 3). Placement ends there per
affect budget, with a guaranteed success as the closing item ("a ruler
reads 7.8 cm — quick, what's that in mm?" → success).

**First teaching session**:  
Category 1: SI unit system, dimensional analysis as the core concept.
Standard teaching flow (not first-lesson, since the learner has prior
physics exposure — RECOGNIZED rung on most concepts, not UNKNOWN).
Entry point: DEMONSTRATION, not INTRODUCTION, because "I've seen units
before" (rung 1, fast anchor possible).

## Student C — Returns After One Year (Mathematics)

**Prior state**: Frontier at Category 8 (intermediate algebra: linear
equations and systems)  
**Gap**: 14 months  
**Expected memory status**: FORGOTTEN for Category 7–8 gate concepts;
STABLE for Categories 1–5 (well-consolidated)

**Phase 1 — Re-placement from high-water mark** (`06 §4`):  
High-water mark: Category 8. Start warm-up there.

**Warm-up attempt — Category 8 gate concepts** (`06 §3`):  
Cued retrieval: "You were working on systems of equations — remember
the substitution method? If y = 2x + 1, and x + y = 10, what would
you do first?" → Blank initially, then slow recovery: "Substitute...
y equals... so x plus 2x plus 1 equals 10?" → Retrieves the method
with prompting. Slow-correct. RECOVERED status begins.

**Cascade begins**: Once Category 8 retrieval starts, the learner begins
offering Category 7 connections unprompted: "Oh right — that's like when
we did the slope thing, right?" Cascade is running. Do NOT interrupt.

**Warm-up result**: Category 8 gate concepts: RECOVERED (slow but correct
with cuing). Category 7 gate concepts: self-recovering via cascade.
Category 6 gate concepts: fluent (were STABLE; unaffected by the gap).

**Placement output**: Frontier = Category 8 (same as before). Category
confidence updates: Categories 1–6: ANCHORED (unchanged). Category 7:
PROBABLE (recovered but not yet re-STABLE). Category 8: UNCERTAIN
(recovered once; needs 2–3 more sessions to reach PROBABLE).

**First teaching session**:  
OPENING: warm-up doubles as review — due items from Categories 7–8
at the session's recovery stage.  
CORE: resume within Category 8 at the frontier concept (the specific
linear system type that was next in the sequence before the gap).  
Expected: 1 session of consolidation before feeling as fluid as before
the gap. History says 2–3 sessions to fully recover to PROBABLE.

**Cascade unlock expectation stated to learner**: "It's coming back
faster than you think it would — storage doesn't go away, it just gets
harder to access for a while. Give it two sessions and it'll feel normal."

## Student D — Expert in Arithmetic, Weak in Fractions (Mathematics)

**Profile**: Categories 1–3 (arithmetic: addition/subtraction/
multiplication/division with whole numbers) ANCHORED. Category 4
(fractions) UNCERTAIN. Category 5+ (beyond fractions) UNKNOWN.  
**Mechanism**: The learner mastered arithmetic procedures but was
absent or disengaged during the fraction unit at school. Fraction
topics are not absent — they are partially known with significant gaps.

**Placement detection**:  
Initial report: "I'm okay at maths but fractions always trip me up."  
Trust: MEDIUM. Domain-specific disclosure is more reliable than a
global level claim.

Category 3 probe (multiplication/division): fast, confident, correct. ✓  
Category 4 probe (fraction equivalence): hesitation, produces 1/2 ≡ 3/6
correctly but slowly. Slow-correct → FRONTIER is here.  
Within-Category 4 probe (adding unlike denominators): produces 1/2 + 1/3
= 2/5. Misconception M1 (add-across) confirmed.

**Placement output**: Categories 1–3: ANCHORED (fast probe, confirmed).
Category 4: UNCERTAIN with active misconception M1 (add-across error).
Category 5+: UNKNOWN.

**Critical rule applied**: The placement engine DOES NOT reteach
Categories 1–3. They appear in the session only as prerequisite anchors
(e.g., "equivalent fractions work the same way as multiplying: 1/2 × 2/2
= 2/4 — the multiplication you already know is running here").

**First teaching session**:  
No warm-up for Categories 1–3 (ANCHORED). Go directly to Category 4.  
But: active misconception M1 means the teaching starts with the
misconception repair track (`../decision-engine/01 §5` locked
configuration), not standard INTRODUCTION. Elicit → commit → collide
using the concept entry's authored sequence
(`../concepts/mathematics/math.arith.fractions.md`).

**Progression path**: Once fraction equivalence + addition reach PROBABLE
(gate concepts at INDEPENDENT, no FRAGILE): advance within Category 4.
Once all Category 4 gate concepts reach AUTOMATIC + STABLE + one
transfer event: Category 4 promotes. Then Category 5 is the new frontier.
Categories 1–3 are never visited as content again.

## Student E — Strong Algebra, Weak Geometry (Mathematics)

**Profile**: Multi-branch, asymmetric. Algebra branch: Category 8
ANCHORED. Geometry branch: Category 3 UNCERTAIN.

**Placement detection** (`03 §7`):  
The spine search finds the algebra frontier advanced (Category 8+).  
Branch probe on geometry: fails at Category 4 (basic geometric reasoning).  
Further branch probe: Category 3 (angle properties) — slow-correct.  
Geometry frontier: Category 3.

**Per-branch placement output**:
```
Algebra:    Category 8 — PROBABLE (working toward ANCHORED via
            delayed-component verification)
Geometry:   Category 3 — UNCERTAIN (one probe; needs confirmation)
Statistics: UNKNOWN (never assessed)
```

**Session planning consequence** (`05 §6`):  
The frontiers do NOT interfere. A session can address either:
- Algebra Category 9 (the next frontier in algebra)
- Geometry Category 3 (verifying and teaching the geometry frontier)
- Not both in the same session — one session, one job

The learner's strong algebra does NOT shorten the geometry work. The
KG's structure shows that geometry and algebra in mathematics share
some prerequisites (both require number sense, both require basic
symbolic reasoning) but are largely independent branches. Algebra
mastery predicts geometry success only at the shared prerequisite level
— it does not replace the geometry-specific work.

**Important implication**: A learner who reports "Advanced in maths"
must be branch-probed. Global "Advanced" in mathematics is a lie in
detail — no learner is advanced across all mathematical branches
simultaneously. The placement engine records per-branch frontiers.

## Student F — Adult Returning Learner with Shame Bias (Mathematics)

**Self-report**: "I'm probably just a beginner — I haven't done maths
in 20 years."  
**Actual level**: Category 3–4 (informal competence in everyday
arithmetic; formal notation absent but concepts intact)  
**Mechanism**: Adult shame bias (first-lesson/01 §4 extended; `02 §1`).
The claim is not dishonest — it is a genuine self-perception shaped by
academic failure in the past and the loss of fluent access after 20 years.

**Phase 1 — Trust model** (`02 §2`):  
Adult + "probably beginner" + 20-year gap = strong under-report prior.
Trust: LOW (40%). Start ONE LEVEL ABOVE the report immediately.
Affect note: this learner has imported shame (`../student-state/06 §3`
imported-history flag); the placement session must be framed as
discovery, not evaluation.

**Phase 2 — Life-competence framing** (`../first-lesson/01 §4`):  
Do NOT use school-mathematics framing. Use informal-competence probes:

Item 1 (below "beginner" in school terms, but in the learner's lived
experience): "Three friends split a restaurant bill of £63 equally.
How much does each person pay?" → Thinks briefly. "£21." Correct.
Confident. "I just divided... yeah, £21." Fast once started.

Item 2 (at informal intermediate level): "You're tiling a bathroom
floor: the room is 3.2 m × 2.5 m, and each tile is 20 cm × 20 cm.
Roughly how many tiles do you need?" → Works it out with unit
awareness. Correct within 10%. Slow but systematic.

Item 3 (approaching formal notation): "A price is reduced by 15%. If
the original price was £80, what is the new price?" → Uses informal
method (10% = £8, 5% = £4, total off = £12, new price = £68). Correct.
No formal percentage formula needed — informal method is correct.

Verdict: informal arithmetic at Category 3–4 level. Formal notation
absent; conceptual competence present. This is the false-beginner
profile (`../first-lesson/01 §4`): vocabulary gap, not knowledge gap.

**Placement output**: Frontier = Category 3 (formal notation and the
decimal/percentage system). Categories 1–2: PROBABLE (informal
competence demonstrated; formal notation unverified, hence PROBABLE
not ANCHORED). No categories ANCHORED yet.

**System behavior**: The placement session ends with "You've got a strong
number sense — it's clearly still there. We're going to build on that
and add the formal notation. That's the piece that makes the rest
accessible." Frame: build on strength. No mention of "20 years away."

**First teaching session**:  
No lesson-one protocol (not a complete beginner — Categories 1–2 are
PROBABLE). Entry at Category 3: use informal competence as the anchor
for formal notation ("you already know that 10% of 80 is 8 — now here's
how mathematicians write that calculation formally").

**Progression note**: This learner's velocity will be fast through
Categories 3–5, because the concepts are already stored informally —
what is being built is the formal notation layer on top of existing
knowledge. Expect cascade recognition across formal notation: each new
symbol maps onto an existing informal concept. The tutor should name
this explicitly: "This is the formal way to write what you've been
doing informally your whole life."
