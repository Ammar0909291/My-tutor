# The Emotional Model

The standing emotional profile — distinct from the momentary affect
states (`../decision-engine/02` owns FEARFUL-now, FRUSTRATED-now). This
model holds what is durably true: what triggers this learner, how fast
they recover, what puts them in flow, what history they imported, and
what actually restores them. The momentary classifier reads its priors
from here; the recovery engine reads its personalization from here.

## 1. Triggers — what reliably causes which state

Recorded per trigger, with evidence: timers ("visible countdowns →
fear, every time"), being assessed by name ("the word 'test' → freeze"),
reading aloud, specific subjects, being watched mid-attempt, unfamiliar
formats, comparison of any kind. Consequences are mechanical: known
triggers are ENGINEERED OUT of this learner's sessions (untimed by
default, checks invisible, formats pre-practiced) — not as coddling but
as validity and affect protection (`../assessment/01 §3` metric 6). A
trigger being worked ON (gradual desensitization — e.g. building timer
tolerance for a real exam goal) is a deliberate, planned exception, never
an accident.

## 2. Recovery speed

How fast affect repairs once triggered — minutes, a session, or days —
per state and per subject. This single number personalizes the Recovery
Engine's pacing: a fast-recoverer gets the standard scripts and the
lesson resumes; a slow-recoverer gets the shortened-session rung
earlier (`../decision-engine/05 §5` — recovery escalates by shrinking),
and their next-session plans pre-shrink after any affect event. Slow
recovery is also the profile marker that converts the standard affect
budget (2 visible failures) into this learner's personal budget of 1
(04 §3's steep-collapse consequence).

## 3. Flow conditions

What gets THIS learner into absorbed, happy work — the challenge level
(just-above vs comfortably-below), the format (games / quiet solo work /
dialogue), the subject, the session moment (some learners need ten
warm-up minutes; some arrive in flow and are ruined by preamble).
Recorded from observed flow episodes (long engaged stretches, resisted
endings, "one more"). Consequence: the planner shapes the CORE toward
known flow conditions, and the tutor protects flow when it appears —
no interruptions, no checks, postpone the plan (flow is rarer and more
valuable than any scheduled item; `../decision-engine/01`'s OBSERVATION
stance holds through it).

## 4. Enjoyment sources and motivational drivers

The persona (Delivery 2 §9) held as an evidence-backed hypothesis, not
a label (LAW 4/5): what has actually energized this learner — beating
their own record, understanding why, finishing things, being trusted
with hard things, teaching back, collecting completions. The close
script's forecast, the stretch framing, and praise style all read this.
Drivers drift with age and season (LAW 2) — re-evidence quarterly.

## 5. Imported history

Subject-specific emotional debt carried in from before this tutor:
"hates maths" with its origin story where known, adult reading shame
(`../first-lesson/01 §6`), the gifted learner's easy-identity fragility
(Delivery 2 §9). Each import is recorded with its externalization
target ("how it was taught", never who they are) and its repair-path
position per subject (FEARFUL → FRAGILE → CONFIDENT,
`../decision-engine/02 §3`). The base-rate rule from LAW 6 stands:
imported dread for mathematics is assumed until disproven.

## 6. Fatigue curve

When quality decays — within sessions (minute N onset, measured, 05 §4)
and across the day/week where visible (evenings bad, Mondays good).
Consequences: scheduling advice surfaces to the learner/family where
channels exist; within sessions, the summit-hunt triggers early on
known short curves.

## 7. What restores this learner (the personalized recovery appendix)

The most operational entry in the whole model: recorded instances of
what actually worked when this learner was flooded — quiet slowing vs
brief humor, a subject switch, tutor-does-it-aloud, mastery evidence
("look at two weeks ago"), a real break, ending early. The Recovery
Engine's scripts are universal; their SELECTION for this learner is
this list. Every recovery event appends to it (what state, what was
tried, what followed) — recovery quality compounds per learner exactly
the way teaching quality compounds per concept.

## 8. Transitions — how the standing model tunes the momentary machine

The profile modulates the weather system's thresholds, in both
directions:

- High fear-trait → the classifier accepts FEARFUL on weaker evidence
  (better a false gentle turn than a missed flood), budgets shrink,
  triggers pre-avoided.
- Robust, fast-recovering profile → the tutor can run closer to the
  edge: harder stretches, longer productive struggle (05 §5's
  persistence budget), rawer feedback — some learners WANT the direct
  version and wilt under cotton wool; over-protection is its own
  mis-teaching, and the model must license robustness as explicitly as
  it flags fragility.
- The repair-path position gates challenge reintroduction per subject:
  stretches return only at CONFIDENT, private wins feed FRAGILE, and
  the model records each crossing — because the learner who has crossed
  once (felt fear → worked anyway → won) owns evidence no speech could
  give them, and the tutor should INVOKE it at the next subject's wall:
  "remember what fractions felt like at the start? You've done this
  climb before."
