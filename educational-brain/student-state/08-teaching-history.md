# Teaching History — What the Tutor Permanently Remembers

The record of what has been TRIED on this learner and what happened. This
is the dimension that makes the second month of tutoring smarter than the
first: without it, every session re-derives the learner from scratch (the
invisible-restart anti-pattern, `../first-lesson/06` AL-16, at the scale
of the whole relationship).

## 1. What is recorded, per concept

- **Explanations that landed** — WHICH frame (mechanism words / story
  words / visual words) and the actual phrasing that worked, verbatim
  where possible. Reused on review ("remember — the bottom number is the
  SIZE") and mined for frame affinity (05 §3).
- **Failed analogies** — never re-offered (the selector's failed-family
  exclusion, `../decision-engine/04` filter 5, needs exactly this data);
  a failed analogy that matches a concept entry's anti-analogy is also
  flagged upstream (authoring confirmation).
- **Best demonstrations** — which demo produced the click; its
  callback value is permanent ("like the coin on the ice — same idea").
- **Action outcomes** — the raw event stream (action family × concept ×
  what followed: advance / struggle / recovery) that 05's affinity
  statistics summarize.
- **Escalation events** — which ladders ran, what rung resolved them
  (`../decision-engine/05` law 3: rung 3+ flags authoring); per learner,
  chronic deep-rung resolution on one concept family is a profile
  finding of its own.
- **Recovery events** — utterance, script used, what actually restored
  them (feeds 06 §7's personalized recovery list).
- **Gate history** — attempts, failures with their autopsied causes
  (`../assessment/09 §1` taxonomy), re-teach counts (2 fails = the
  stuck-concept protocol, `../decision-engine/07 §7`).

## 2. Breakthrough moments — the gold entries

The exact representation, moment, and phrasing that caused a genuine
click, recorded verbatim with context. Two permanent uses:

1. **Callback teaching**: the breakthrough becomes a named shared
   landmark — "remember when the pirate treasure thing suddenly made
   sense? This is that, again" — which is both an anchor (the best
   possible: their own victory) and a relationship deposit
   (`../decision-engine/06 §1` continuity).
2. **Representation-family inheritance**: the family that unlocked one
   concept is the FIRST candidate for its siblings — the number line
   that unlocked negatives leads for inequalities; the fair-share story
   that unlocked fractions leads for division. This inheritance is the
   single cheapest prediction the history makes, and it compounds: each
   breakthrough narrows the search for the next one.

## 3. The summarization policy (append raw, read summaries)

The history is append-only, but the tutor recalls PATTERNS, not
turn-by-turn logs. Three layers:

```
RAW EVENTS      every action outcome, escalation, recovery, gate —
                append-only, dated (the audit trail and the
                re-derivation source)
STATISTICS      the rollups the profiles read: affinity counts (05),
                calibration series (04), forgetting rate (07),
                trajectory series (09)
PROFILE ENTRIES the standing sentences with evidence pointers —
                what the tutor "just knows" about this learner
```

Two classes of events are exempt from summarization and kept verbatim
forever: **breakthroughs** (§2 — they are future scripts) and
**severe negative events** (a humiliation, a session that ended in
tears, an "I'm stupid" — because the tutor must never accidentally
recreate their circumstances, and because their anniversary shadows
are real: the learner remembers even when the summary wouldn't).

## 4. History-reading rules

- **Recency-weighted, never recency-only**: last month's outcomes
  outvote last season's (LAW 2), but a verbatim breakthrough never
  expires.
- **The history disciplines optimism**: before any plan says "let's
  try X", the history answers "X was tried on this concept twice;
  rung-2 escalations both times." The selector's exclusions (filter 5)
  are this rule mechanized.
- **The history is the moat's per-learner half**: the concept entries
  accumulate what works for EVERYONE (via the feedback loop,
  `../first-lesson/08`); this history accumulates what works for THIS
  ONE. World-class tutoring is the intersection — retrieved from both,
  invented from neither.
