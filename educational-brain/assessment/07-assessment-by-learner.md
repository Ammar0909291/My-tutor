# Assessment by Learner: Level, Age, Subject, Persona

The instruments are universal; the *deployment* adapts to who is being
assessed. Adaptation changes framing, density, format, and feedback timing —
never the evidence standards themselves. A beginner's mastery gate and an
expert's mastery gate certify the same six properties (05 §1); they differ in
how the evidence is gathered, not in what counts as evidence.

## 1. By level

### Beginner (in this concept region)

- **High success density**: ~80–90% of items answered correctly, by design.
  Early failure at high rates teaches "this subject isn't for me" before any
  content is learned. The information cost is acceptable: at this stage the
  tutor mostly needs to detect gross gaps, which sparse failures reveal fine.
- **Recognition → cued production → production**, in that order, as the item
  diet. Recognition items are legitimate *here* (nowhere else) because the
  goal is building, not certifying.
- **Immediate feedback**, warm and informational. Delayed feedback benefits
  retention for established learners, but beginners with no self-check
  ability practice errors during the delay.
- **Taught-adjacent surfaces first**: transfer distance ramps only after the
  base representation is stable. Far transfer on day one measures nothing
  (there is nothing to transfer yet).
- **No trick surfaces, no mixed sets yet.** Discrimination among concepts
  requires more than one stable concept to discriminate between.

### Intermediate

- **Production is the default**; recognition retires.
- **Mixed sets begin** — the noticing skill (05 §2, level 7) starts here,
  announced at first ("these mix the last three ideas"), unannounced later.
- **Transfer distance grows deliberately**: new surfaces, then new
  representations.
- **Feedback starts to delay**: "commit to all three answers, then we
  check" — building self-checking before external checking.
- **Two-tier items appear** (03 §3): right-answer-wrong-reason is the
  characteristic intermediate pathology.

### Advanced

- **Unannounced mixing is the norm**; every item begins with "which concept
  is this?" implicitly.
- **Generative assessment**: "construct an example", "construct a
  counterexample", "build an item that would catch someone who believes X".
  Generation is a harder and more honest test than solution — you cannot
  construct a counterexample to a boundary you can't see.
- **Error analysis as assessment** (Delivery 2 §6): "here is a wrong
  solution — find the flaw, name the misconception behind it."
- **Edge cases and boundary probes**: advanced knowledge *is* knowledge of
  boundaries (mental model stage 4, Delivery 2 §1).
- **Self-assessment duties**: the learner predicts their own gate results
  before taking them; calibration (04) becomes an explicit target.

## 2. By age

### Early childhood (~4–7)

- **Assessment must be invisible.** Embedded in play, conversation, and
  games; the word "test" never occurs; no visible scoring. Observation
  during play outperforms any item format.
- **Oral + manipulative only.** No written formats; pointing, sorting
  physical objects, acting out, and the confused-puppet technique (06 §1).
- **30–60 second bursts**, woven into activity — sustained assessment blocks
  don't exist at this age.
- **Behavioral confidence signals only** (04 §2): self-report is
  developmentally unavailable.
- Every session ends with success, visibly celebrated (process-praise).

### Primary (~7–11)

- **Game and quest framing** carries assessment without disguising learning:
  points for effort and strategy, not just correctness.
- **Concrete materials remain first-class**: assessment with blocks, number
  lines, and drawings is not "easier" assessment — at this age it is the
  valid format; premature symbol-only assessment measures symbol fluency.
- **Short written formats begin**, introduced with throwaway practice items
  (format unfamiliarity, 01 §4).
- **Immediate warm feedback**; error-as-information framing established
  now ("mistakes tell us what to do next") pays for a decade.

### Adolescent (~11–17)

- **Private results, always.** Social exposure is the dominant affect risk;
  no public scores, no visible comparisons, opt-in competition only.
- **Relevance and respect**: assessment framed as *their* progress data,
  not surveillance. Share the why of formats and schedules — adolescents
  comply with what they understand and quietly sabotage what they don't.
- **Exam-format stamina building** where the learner has real exam goals:
  format familiarity, timing tolerance, and mark-scheme literacy are
  legitimate assessment-adjacent skills — trained *separately* from concept
  assessment so neither corrupts the other.

### Adult

- **Transparent purpose**: adults get the full reasoning — what this check
  is for, what happens with the result. Opaque assessment reads as
  infantilizing and is quietly resented.
- **Self-assessment first, then verify**: "where do you think you are on
  this?" respects autonomy and simultaneously collects calibration data
  (02 §5, 04 §3).
- **Dense, low-count item sets**: respect for time is respect. Three
  well-chosen items, not fifteen.
- **Rust-sensitive interpretation**: adult failure is often decayed
  retrieval of once-solid knowledge, not absence — one cued-retrieval item
  distinguishes them (fast relearning confirms rust; see Delivery 2 §8 on
  storage strength), and the distinction transforms the message: "it's
  there, it's just unoiled" vs. silently re-teaching from zero.

## 3. By subject

- **Mathematics**: production items with visible work; distractors sourced
  from the documented procedural-bug and misconception libraries (03 §2);
  two-tier "why" items against rule-without-reason; number-choice discipline
  in templates (03 §4 — slot values change difficulty); representation
  translation (equation ↔ graph ↔ words) as a standing gate component.
- **Reading / language**: decoding and pronunciation are voice-required
  (06 §1); comprehension assessed by retell + inference questions, never
  recall-only ("what color was the door?" measures attention, not
  comprehension — "why did she hide the letter?" measures the model of the
  story); production is the only test of expression ("say/write your own
  sentence using...") — recognition of grammar is not command of it;
  nonsense words remain the decoding golden probe.
- **Sciences**: prediction items *before* demonstrations (the assessment is
  the prediction; 08 §4); explanation items scored for mechanism (10 §2);
  the richest documented misconception literatures in existence — distractor
  sets should lean on them hard; practical rubrics for anything involving
  hands (06 §3); "design a test for..." as the advanced generative format
  (08 §7).
- **Programming and CS-adjacent skills**: read-trace-predict before write
  ("what does this output?" is the prediction probe of code); debugging
  items are error analysis in native form; writing from scratch is the
  production gate; style and decomposition assessed by rubric, never by
  single items.

## 4. By persona (assessment-specific adaptations of Delivery 2 §9)

- **Fearful**: untimed, private, framed as practice ("let's warm up with a
  few"), success-first ordering, affect budget halved (one visible failure,
  not two). The fearful learner's data is the most corruption-prone —
  anxiety floods working memory — so *calm is a validity requirement*, not
  a kindness.
- **Perfectionist**: error-hunting items ("find what's wrong here") let them
  meet errors as objects, not verdicts; never frame a gate as
  all-or-nothing; pre-announce that gates are repeatable by design.
- **Competitive**: personal-record framing ("beat your own last set");
  wager-style confidence elicitation (04 §2); guard the known trap —
  metric-gaming — with transfer items, which cannot be gamed.
- **Exam-oriented**: mirror the target exam's formats in *stamina training*
  sessions while keeping diagnostic assessment format-free; show coverage
  maps; sell retrieval checks in their currency (score protection —
  truthfully).
- **Unmotivated / burned-out**: minimum viable assessment — micro-diagnosis
  through conversation only (02 §7); zero formal checks until re-engagement;
  no streak or completion pressure attached to assessment of any kind.
- **Gifted**: compaction pretests ("test out" opportunities) are respectful
  and efficient — but their gates still require the delayed and transfer
  components; speed of first success is exactly the evidence that does NOT
  certify durability (05 §1).
