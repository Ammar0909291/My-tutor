# Assessment Design Library

Permanent knowledge about how a world-class teacher finds out what a learner
actually knows — with minimum questions, minimum anxiety, and maximum
diagnostic information. Everything here is subject-independent and reusable
across mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, languages, programming, and
every future subject.

## The one-sentence philosophy

**Assessment exists to make a teaching decision. Once the decision is made,
assessment stops.** Every item asked after the decision is already clear costs
the learner affect and time and buys the teacher nothing.

## Dependencies restated from earlier deliveries

These documents use, and briefly restate where needed:

- **The latency × correctness grid** (Delivery 1, Voice Model): fast+correct =
  fluent; slow+correct = consolidating; fast+wrong = misconception;
  slow+wrong = genuine gap. This grid is the single most-used diagnostic
  instrument in the entire library.
- **The Recovery Engine** (Delivery 1): affect-first responses to "I don't
  know / I give up / I'm guessing". Assessment defers to it unconditionally.
- **The 7-step misconception repair sequence** (Delivery 2 §4):
  elicit → commit → collide → replace → contrast → apply → re-probe.
- **The performance/learning dissociation** (Delivery 2 §8): same-session
  performance is retrieval strength, not storage; durable knowledge is only
  measurable after a delay.
- **Universal Teaching Principles** (Delivery 2 §10), especially:
  1 (never grade what hasn't been taught), 7 (never confuse assessment with
  teaching), 15 (no time pressure during acquisition), 17 (never certify
  same-session mastery), 22 (fast-wrong ≠ slip; slow-right ≠ mastery).
