# DO Family

Actions where the learner produces something rather than observes or is
told something. DO is the family Universal Principle 19
(`../foundations/04-universal-teaching-principles.md`) exists to protect:
"the learner doing beats the learner watching, on every tie" — whenever
a SHOW or TELL action and a DO action would fit equally well, DO wins
(`../decision-engine/04` filter 7b). DO actions are also the family
Filter 6 (load fit) restricts hardest under any load signal — production
costs more working memory than reception, so this family narrows first
when headroom is limited.

## 10. Completion Problem

**What it is**: a partially-worked problem where the learner finishes
the remaining steps — the fading bridge between a Worked Example
(`01-show-family.md #2`) and full Faded Practice (#11 below).

**Fits**: procedure.

**Setup cost**: MEDIUM — requires a genuine worked-example/completion
pair prepared in advance, not an improvised cut-off mid-explanation.

**Procedure**: show the first N steps solved, ask the learner to
complete the remaining steps; the fraction completed by the learner
grows across a session as fluency increases (the fading ladder,
`../decision-engine/01` GUIDED's echo→supported→prompted sub-steps
applied at problem scale).

**Failure mode**: skipping straight from a full Worked Example to full
Faded Practice with no completion stage in between — the jump in demand
is too large for a learner not yet at the top of the GUIDED sub-ladder.

## 11. Faded Practice

**What it is**: the learner solves complete instances alone, with
support fading across repetitions — echo (learner repeats alongside
tutor) → supported (tutor available on request) → prompted (tutor cues
only on struggle) → independent solo.

**Fits**: procedure — this is the default DO action for any procedural
skill once Completion Problems have been exhausted.

**Setup cost**: MEDIUM — needs a bank of isomorphic items at controlled
difficulty, not ad hoc improvised problems (identical-item repetition is
banned; `../assessment/09 §4`).

**Procedure**: advance the fade level only on the fluency gate (fast +
correct + confident, three in a row — `../foundations/02-adaptive-
teaching-rules.md §2`), never on correctness alone. A slow-correct
response at any fade level holds; it does not advance the fade.

**Failure mode**: advancing the fade level on a single correct answer
regardless of speed or confidence — the single most common premature-
advancement error, directly prohibited by Universal Principle 3.

## 12. Drawing

**What it is**: the learner produces a diagram, sketch, or visual
representation of the concept or mechanism — "the model X-ray"
(`../assessment/06 §2`), because what a learner draws reveals the model
they are actually running, which speech can conceal.

**Fits**: concept, causal system (`../decision-engine/04` filter 2's
"concept → ... drawing" row).

**Setup cost**: MEDIUM — needs a directed prompt ("draw what's happening
inside the wire when the current flows") rather than an open "draw a
picture," which produces decoration instead of a probe-able artifact.

**Procedure**: give the directed prompt, let the learner draw without
interruption, then use the drawing as a probe: "walk me through what
you've drawn" — the walkthrough often surfaces the misconception the
drawing encodes even when the learner's verbal description would have
sounded correct.

**Dual role**: Drawing is simultaneously a teaching action and an
assessment instrument (`../assessment/06 §2`) — it is one of the few
actions in this catalog that does both at once, which Filter 7d rewards
("the form that doubles as evidence beats the form that doesn't").

## 13. Enactment

**What it is**: the learner physically performs the procedure with
their own hands or body — required, not optional, for physical
procedures (motor skills, pronunciation, handwriting) where watching or
talking about the action cannot substitute for doing it.

**Fits**: physical procedure — the canonical, often only, legal action
for this knowledge type (`../decision-engine/04` filter 2: "physical
procedure → practical/enactment (no substitute)").

**Setup cost**: HIGH — requires physical space, materials, or the
learner's active physical participation, which is harder to arrange and
higher-stakes to fail in front of others than any verbal action.

**Persona note**: no public role-play form of enactment for fearful
learners (`../decision-engine/04` filter 4) — enactment for this persona
should be private (with the tutor only, not observed by peers) or object-
mediated (puppet/manipulative standing in for the learner's own body,
which lowers the exposure). Puppet and object-mediated forms of
enactment fade in preference for adolescents, who typically prefer
private variants over anything visibly performative.

**Failure mode**: substituting a verbal description or a watched
demonstration for enactment on a genuinely physical procedure — the
learner can describe correct pronunciation perfectly and still be unable
to produce it; only enactment tests and builds the actual motor skill.

## 14. Simulation-Manipulated

**What it is**: the learner directly controls the parameters of a
dynamic system and observes the consequences of their own choices — the
learner-controlled form of Simulation; see `01-show-family.md #4` for the
watched form.

**Fits**: causal system.

**Setup cost**: HIGH — parameter spaces are load, and handing control to
the learner multiplies that load by adding decision-making about WHICH
parameters to try (`../decision-engine/04` filter 6: "no simulations...
no multi-step games, no model-building" under any load signal). Reserve
for full-headroom, drive-band states (CONFIDENT, CURIOUS) only.

**Procedure**: after Simulation-Observed has established the basic
mechanism, hand over one parameter at a time — never the full parameter
space at once — and let the learner form and test their own hypotheses
about what changes.

**Failure mode**: handing over full manipulation before the learner has
watched the system run at all (skipping Simulation-Observed) — the
learner then spends their effort discovering the interface and the basic
mechanism simultaneously, which is exactly the compounded-load pattern
this family must avoid.
