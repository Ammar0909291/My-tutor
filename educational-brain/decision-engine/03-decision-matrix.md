# The Decision Matrix — Teaching State × Student State → Decision

Concrete decisions, not recommendations. The full cross-product would be
~180 cells, most reading "go to recovery" — repetition dressed as rigor.
The honest structure of tutoring collapses it into **one preemption rule
plus a compact matrix**, and that collapse is itself the first piece of
decision intelligence:

## 0. The preemption rule (replaces five rows of the matrix)

> If the student state is in the affect band — FEARFUL, FRUSTRATED,
> OVERLOADED (severe), DISENGAGED, FATIGUED — the teaching state is
> irrelevant: **preempt to RECOVERY** (01 §4), whose interior scripts are
> the Recovery Engine. The one nuance the teaching state adds is the
> re-entry point (one step below where recovery fired) and, for FATIGUED,
> the destination (CLOSING via a win, not re-entry).

Everything below therefore covers the cognitive and drive bands:
CONFUSED · GUESSING · MISCONCEIVING · FRAGILE · CONFIDENT · CURIOUS · BORED.

## 1. During INTRODUCTION (the anchor)

- **CONFUSED** → abandon this anchor mid-sentence; try ONE different
  anchor from the concept entry's library; if that lands nowhere,
  demonstrate anyway — the experience will do what the words couldn't.
- **GUESSING** → you asked something during an anchor; stop — anchors
  contain no questions. Statement form, then demonstrate.
- **MISCONCEIVING** (the anchor woke a documented misconception — learner
  says its characteristic phrase) → jackpot, not a problem: log it, and
  convert the lesson's demonstration into the misconception's collision
  case (the concept entry names it). The lesson just got better.
- **FRAGILE / CONFUSED about the PREREQUISITE the anchor leaned on** →
  the anchor found a floor problem; one cued-retrieval rep of the
  prerequisite now; if it doesn't come back cued, switch the lesson to
  the prerequisite (07 owns the plan swap; the learner hears "let's
  sharpen the tool first — this'll take five minutes").
- **CONFIDENT** → shortest possible anchor, or skip to demonstration;
  confidence at introduction means the anchor is a formality.
- **CURIOUS** → let their question BE the anchor ("that exact question is
  today's lesson") — the strongest opening that exists; reorder freely.
- **BORED** → skip ceremony entirely; open with the most surprising
  version available (the concept entry's curiosity hook / a
  confidently-wrong-setup item).

## 2. During DEMONSTRATION

- **CONFUSED** → slow the same demo down and exaggerate the critical
  moment; narrate one beat ("watch RIGHT here"). Do not switch demos yet
  — confusion at first viewing is normal (the heartbeat, 02 §3).
- **GUESSING** (on the prediction ask) → fine — predictions are allowed
  to be guesses; say so ("a guess is exactly right here") and run the
  reveal. Prediction-guessing is free; it still creates the stake.
- **MISCONCEIVING** (prediction states the documented wrong model) →
  perfect setup: commit them to it warmly ("lock that in — let's see"),
  reveal, then let the mismatch sit in silence for a full beat before a
  word is said (Delivery 2 §4 step 3 — the silence does the teaching).
- **FRAGILE** → one more instance than planned (three, not two), inviting
  a co-prediction on the last ("call it with me").
- **CONFIDENT** → cut to one instance and hand them the apparatus:
  "you run it — surprise me." Demonstration becomes their production.
- **CURIOUS** → run their "what if" as instance two, even if unplanned —
  a simulation-shaped question deserves a simulation-shaped answer.
- **BORED** → give the extreme case first (the weirdest parameter value),
  not the canonical one; canonical bores the bored.

## 3. During EXPLANATION

- **CONFUSED** → stop the sentence; back to DEMONSTRATION (transition
  table — never a second verbal pass in the same frame). If this is
  already the post-demo explanation: change the FRAME (visual words →
  story words), one sentence, once. Then it's the escalation ladder's
  business (05 §1).
- **GUESSING / MISCONCEIVING / FRAGILE** → explanation is the wrong state
  for all three (they only surface on questions, and explanation asks
  none) — their appearance means a question slipped in; close it, finish
  the burst, move to GUIDED where these states are handled natively.
- **CONFIDENT** → compress: give the expert's one-liner and go straight
  to practice; confident learners are load-bearing-sentence learners.
- **CURIOUS** → answer the question they're visibly forming before the
  invitation ("and you're wondering why — here's why"), which converts
  exposition into resolution (Delivery 2 §3: information as answer to a
  felt question).
- **BORED** → stop explaining entirely (the material is beneath them or
  the burst ran long); jump to the hardest legal practice item.

## 4. During GUIDED PRACTICE

- **CONFUSED** → one sub-step down, silently; re-model the exact
  difficulty point (model-again beats explain-the-error at every level,
  not just lesson one — `../first-lesson/03 §6` generalizes).
- **GUESSING** → reward the honesty, then shrink the ask to the largest
  step they can do with certainty: recognition or echo form of the same
  item. Log: the sub-step advanced too early.
- **MISCONCEIVING** → do NOT correct mid-practice more than once; after
  one redirect, mark the misconception for its own dedicated pass
  (Delivery 2 §4) and steer practice to items the misconception doesn't
  contaminate — practicing INTO a live misconception rehearses it.
- **FRAGILE** → hold the sub-step; one more success at the same level,
  then one more; advance only on the fluency signal, and say what you
  see ("that one was quicker — feel it?" — fluency made audible builds
  calibration, `../assessment/04 §4`).
- **CONFIDENT** → skip sub-steps; jump to prompted or straight to a solo.
  Guided practice for a confident learner is redundancy (expertise
  reversal, Delivery 2 §5).
- **CURIOUS** → allow one "what happens if I do it wrong on purpose?"
  detour — deliberate-error play is high-grade learning (error analysis
  in native form) — then back to the ladder.
- **BORED** → practice variety, not more reps: same skill, weirder
  surface (the concept entry's far-transfer contexts pulled forward).

## 5. During INDEPENDENT PRACTICE

- **CONFUSED** → back one sub-step to GUIDED, zero commentary (the
  backward arrow is normal motion, 01 §3).
- **GUESSING** → the solo was premature; back to GUIDED-prompted, and
  the next solo attempt gets a lighter item first.
- **MISCONCEIVING** (fast-wrong solo) → the strongest detection moment
  there is: capture the exact utterance for the misconception ledger,
  respond with curiosity not correction ("how did you get that?" — same
  tone as for a right answer, `../assessment/09 §2`), and schedule the
  dedicated repair pass. Do not resolve it inline.
- **FRAGILE** (slow-correct solo) → banked, but not advanced: one more
  solo of the same type NOW (converting one shaky success into two solid
  ones — Delivery 1), and the gate's fluency component stays unmet.
- **CONFIDENT** → raise transfer distance on the next rep (new surface,
  then new representation — the gate set is being built live,
  `../assessment/05 §3`).
- **CURIOUS** → offer the self-test: "want to try the weird version?"
  (stretch item inside the concept) — curiosity at independence is the
  learner asking to be gated.
- **BORED** → stop practicing; the reps are done or the item bank is too
  easy — move to ASSESSMENT or TRANSITION now.

## 6. During ASSESSMENT

The interior is owned by `../assessment/05 §4` (next-item policy) and
`../assessment/09` (failure). The matrix adds only the state-reads:

- **GUESSING** → confidence elicitation is now mandatory per item
  (`../assessment/04 §2`) and the stop rule tightens: a guessed pass
  decides nothing, so don't count it.
- **MISCONCEIVING** → switch from difficulty-measurement to the
  misconception's dedicated probe set (the "which model" question has
  replaced the "how much" question — 05 §4 of assessment).
- **FRAGILE** → assessment pauses; consolidation items are practice, not
  measurement; resume the gate another day (delayed component exists
  anyway).
- **CONFIDENT/CURIOUS/BORED** → proceed; for BORED, compress to the
  minimum decisive item set (respect for time IS the treatment).

## 7. During REVIEW

- **FORGOTTEN** → cued retrieval ladder (cue → partial → regenerate),
  never re-teach; expect the cascade (02 §1).
- **FRAGILE** → interval shrinks one step, say nothing about it.
- **CONFIDENT + fast** → interval expands; one sentence of made-visible
  progress ("three weeks ago this took you a minute — watch yourself
  now").
- **BORED** → reviews get game-framing and mixing (interleaved subjects),
  never abandonment — bored learners still forget; the schedule is
  non-negotiable, its clothing isn't.
- **MISCONCEIVING on a previously-repaired item** → the weed regrew
  (expected — Delivery 2 §4): re-run the SHORT form of the repair
  (re-collide with the original anomaly, re-contrast), reset that item's
  interval to dense, and log the regrowth in the concept's ledger.

## 8. During TRANSITION / CLOSING

- **CURIOUS** → plant the open loop on their live question (the close's
  loop writes itself).
- **FATIGUED / any affect state** → shortest legal close: the named win +
  "same time tomorrow" — skip the forecast detail, never skip the named
  win.
- **CONFIDENT** → forecast names the next CHALLENGE, not the next topic
  ("tomorrow's one is meaner — you're ready"), which converts closing
  into an appetite.
- **BORED** → the close names what was SKIPPED as the achievement
  ("you cleared in one day what's planned for three") — pace itself
  becomes the reward.
