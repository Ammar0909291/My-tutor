## Component 1 — Misconception Register

### MC-ANY-TRANSITION-WORD-CAN-BE-INSERTED-ANYWHERE-TO-IMPROVE-FLOW
- **Trigger signal:** Student inserts transition words ("however," "therefore," "in addition") somewhat randomly between sentences to make writing "sound more connected," without checking whether the specific logical relationship signaled by that transition word actually matches the relationship between the two ideas — producing transitions that create false or confusing logical connections.
- **Conflict evidence [P28]:** Present "The team practiced for months. However, they won the championship." (incorrect — "however" signals contrast/contradiction, but winning after practicing is a logical CONSEQUENCE, not a contradiction) alongside "The team practiced for months. As a result, they won the championship." (correct — "as a result" signals the actual cause-effect relationship present). Ask the student which transition accurately represents the relationship between the two sentences.
- **Bridge text [P30]:** "Transition words aren't just flow-improving decorations you can sprinkle anywhere — each one signals a SPECIFIC logical relationship (contrast, cause-effect, addition, sequence, example). Using the wrong transition doesn't just sound slightly off; it actively misrepresents the logical relationship between your ideas, confusing the reader about how the ideas actually connect."
- **Replacement text [P31]:** "Before inserting a transition word, identify the actual logical relationship between the two ideas: are they contrasting, is one causing the other, is one adding to the other, are they in sequence? Then choose the transition word that accurately signals THAT specific relationship, not just any word that sounds smooth."
- **Discrimination pairs [P33]:** (a) Transition-as-decoration (any transition word improves flow regardless of the actual logical relationship) vs. (b) Transition-as-logical-signal (each transition word signals a specific logical relationship — contrast, cause-effect, addition, sequence — that must accurately match the relationship actually present between the ideas).
- **s6_path:** If detected, drop to PD-1 re-check, then re-run TA-1's relationship-identification-first practice before continuing.

### MC-COHESION-ONLY-HAPPENS-AT-THE-BEGINNING-OF-SENTENCES-WITH-TRANSITION-WORDS
- **Trigger signal:** Student believes cohesion is achieved solely by placing transition words at the start of sentences, overlooking other cohesive devices (pronoun reference back to previous nouns, repeated key terms, synonym substitution to avoid repetition while maintaining the connection) that create flow within and across sentences without needing an explicit sentence-starting transition word every time.
- **Conflict evidence [P28]:** Present a paragraph achieving strong cohesion primarily through consistent pronoun reference and repeated key terms, with almost no sentence-starting transition words at all (e.g., "The volcano erupted violently. It sent ash miles into the sky. The ash cloud eventually drifted over three countries.") Ask the student whether this paragraph flows well and connects ideas despite having very few explicit transition words.
- **Bridge text [P30]:** "Transition words at sentence starts are only ONE tool for cohesion, not the only one. Pronouns referring back to previous nouns ('it,' 'this,' 'these'), repeated key terms, and synonym substitution all create flow and connection between sentences too — often more naturally than stacking transition words at every sentence's start."
- **Replacement text [P31]:** "Don't rely only on sentence-starting transition words for cohesion. Also use pronouns that clearly refer back to something just mentioned, repeat key terms when helpful for clarity, and vary your language with synonyms while keeping the connection clear. A paragraph can be highly cohesive with very few explicit transition words."
- **Discrimination pairs [P33]:** (a) Transition-words-only cohesion (cohesion is achieved solely through explicit sentence-starting transition words) vs. (b) Multi-device cohesion (cohesion is achieved through several devices — transition words, pronoun reference, repeated key terms, synonym substitution — used as appropriate, not just one).
- **s6_path:** If detected, drop to PD-1, then re-present the Concrete Anchor's multi-tool toolbox framing before continuing to TA-3.

## Component 2 — Prerequisite Diagnostic Block

### PD-1: Supporting-Details Readiness
- **Tests:** Can the student already select and order well-chosen supporting details within a paragraph — the material transitions and cohesion devices connect smoothly?
- **Probe:** Ask the student to identify whether a given set of details is well-ordered and well-selected in a sample paragraph.
- **Pass condition:** Reasonable judgment about detail order/selection.
- **Fail routing:** If detail organization is weak, route to `eng.writing.supporting-details` review — there must be well-ordered content before transitions can meaningfully connect it.

## Component 3 — Concrete Anchor [P06]

**"The Logical Relationship Toolbox."** Present 5 physical "connector tool" cards, each labeled with its logical job and matching transition words: **Contrast Tool** ("however," "on the other hand," "despite this"), **Cause-Effect Tool** ("therefore," "as a result," "consequently"), **Addition Tool** ("furthermore," "in addition," "moreover"), **Sequence Tool** ("first," "next," "finally"), **Example Tool** ("for instance," "specifically," "for example"). Present two sentences and have the student first determine the ACTUAL logical relationship between them before picking up the matching tool card — never picking a tool just because it "sounds nice," directly seeding MC-ANY-TRANSITION-WORD-CAN-BE-INSERTED-ANYWHERE-TO-IMPROVE-FLOW's fix. Then introduce 3 additional non-tool "connector types" that don't use transition words at all: a Pronoun Thread (a string literally connecting a noun in sentence 1 to a pronoun in sentence 2), a Repeated-Term Anchor (the same key word appearing in both sentences), and a Synonym Bridge (a different word with the same meaning linking two sentences) — demonstrating cohesion works through multiple devices, not transition words alone, seeding MC-COHESION-ONLY-HAPPENS-AT-THE-BEGINNING-OF-SENTENCES-WITH-TRANSITION-WORDS's fix.

## Component 4 — Conceptual Development Sequence

### TA-1: Identifying the Actual Logical Relationship First
Present sentence pairs and have the student identify the actual relationship (contrast, cause-effect, addition, sequence, example) BEFORE selecting a transition word, using the toolbox categories. Directly counters MC-ANY-TRANSITION-WORD-CAN-BE-INSERTED-ANYWHERE-TO-IMPROVE-FLOW.

### TA-2: Matching Transition Words to Relationships
Given a relationship type, student generates 2-3 correct transition word options and practices inserting the correctly-matched transition into 4 sentence pairs.

### TA-3: Non-Transition-Word Cohesive Devices
Using the discrimination pairs from MC-COHESION-ONLY-HAPPENS-AT-THE-BEGINNING-OF-SENTENCES-WITH-TRANSITION-WORDS, present paragraphs achieving cohesion through pronoun reference, repeated terms, and synonym substitution (with minimal transition words) and have the student identify each cohesive device at work.

### TA-4: Detecting a Mismatched Transition
Present sentences with an incorrectly-matched transition word (as in the Conflict Evidence example) and have the student identify the mismatch and correct it to the appropriate transition for the actual relationship present.

### TA-5: Composing a Fully Cohesive Multi-Paragraph Passage
Student writes a short multi-paragraph passage using a deliberate variety of cohesive devices (correctly-matched transitions, pronoun reference, repeated terms, synonym substitution) rather than relying on one device alone — the generative capstone.

## Component 5 — Worked Examples

### WE-1: Relationship-First Transition Selection (Scaffolded)
**Sentences:** "The bridge was closed for repairs." / "Traffic backed up for miles."
**Walkthrough:** Identify the relationship as cause-effect (the closure caused the backup), then select "as a result" or "consequently" rather than an unrelated transition like "however" or "for example."

### WE-2: Non-Transition Cohesive Devices (Guided)
**Passage:** "Marie Curie discovered radium in 1898. Her work with this element eventually led to major advances in cancer treatment. The scientist's discovery remains one of the most significant in medical history."
**Walkthrough:** Identify "this element" (synonym/reference bridge to "radium"), "her work" (pronoun reference to Marie Curie), and "the scientist's discovery" (synonym substitution for Marie Curie/her radium work) as cohesive devices connecting the sentences without needing sentence-starting transition words.

### WE-3: Mismatched Transition Correction (Independent)
**Sentence pair with error:** "She studied for weeks. In addition, she failed the exam."
**Walkthrough:** Student identifies "in addition" as mismatched (this signals addition, but the actual relationship is contrast/unexpected outcome) and corrects it to "however" or "despite this," which accurately signals the contrast between studying hard and still failing.

## Component 6 — Mastery Probe Set

**MP-1 [P36] — Select a transition matching the actual relationship**
Given a sentence pair, student identifies the actual logical relationship and selects an accurately-matched transition word, rather than any word that merely "sounds smooth." *(Directly targets MC-ANY-TRANSITION-WORD-CAN-BE-INSERTED-ANYWHERE-TO-IMPROVE-FLOW.)*

**MP-2 [P36] — Identify non-transition cohesive devices**
Given a paragraph with minimal transition words but strong cohesion through pronoun reference/repeated terms/synonyms, student identifies these devices and explains how they create flow. *(Directly targets MC-COHESION-ONLY-HAPPENS-AT-THE-BEGINNING-OF-SENTENCES-WITH-TRANSITION-WORDS.)*

**MP-3 [P36] — Detect and correct a mismatched transition**
Given a sentence pair with an incorrectly-matched transition word, student identifies the mismatch and corrects it.

**MP-4 [P36] — Generate relationship-matched transitions**
Given 4 sentence pairs with different underlying relationships, student generates a correctly-matched transition for each.

**MP-5 [P36] — Compose a multi-device cohesive passage**
Student writes a short passage using a deliberate variety of cohesive devices (transitions, pronoun reference, repeated terms, synonyms).

**Protocol routing paragraph:** At session open (S0), confirm PD-1 passes — transitions and cohesion connect already-organized supporting details, so weak detail organization undermines what there is to connect. During instruction (S1), watch for both misconception patterns: inserting transitions without checking actual logical fit (MC-ANY-TRANSITION-WORD-CAN-BE-INSERTED-ANYWHERE-TO-IMPROVE-FLOW) and relying exclusively on transition words for cohesion (MC-COHESION-ONLY-HAPPENS-AT-THE-BEGINNING-OF-SENTENCES-WITH-TRANSITION-WORDS) both under-use the full toolkit available — redirect both to the Logical Relationship Toolbox anchor. If the student inserts a mismatched transition or ignores non-transition cohesive devices during TA-1/TA-3 (S6), drop back to the Concrete Anchor's relationship-identification-first practice before returning to composition. At mastery-probe stage (S9), do not certify mastery unless MP-1 and MP-2 both pass — a student who generates matched transitions correctly (MP-4) but cannot detect a mismatch or recognize non-transition cohesion has not yet learned the full judgment this concept requires.

## Component 8 — Adaptive Flags

- If the student inserts transitions without checking the actual logical relationship, weight remediation toward MC-ANY-TRANSITION-WORD-CAN-BE-INSERTED-ANYWHERE-TO-IMPROVE-FLOW's bridge text and re-run TA-1's relationship-identification-first practice before returning to transition selection.
- If the student relies exclusively on transition words and ignores other cohesive devices, weight remediation toward MC-COHESION-ONLY-HAPPENS-AT-THE-BEGINNING-OF-SENTENCES-WITH-TRANSITION-WORDS's bridge text and TA-3's non-transition-device practice specifically.
- This concept feeds four direct downstream unlocks (`eng.writing.narrative-writing`, `eng.writing.descriptive-writing`, `eng.writing.expository-writing`, `eng.writing.persuasive-writing-basics`) — do not certify mastery without TA-5's multi-device composition, since all four downstream genre-specific writing concepts assume the student can already achieve cohesion using a full toolkit, not just transition words.
- Reuse the "logical relationship toolbox" framing explicitly across all four downstream writing genres — each genre relies on the same relationship-first transition selection, applied to genre-specific content (narrative sequence, descriptive addition, expository cause-effect, persuasive contrast/counterargument).
